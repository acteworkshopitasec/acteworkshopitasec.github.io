Call for Abstract

To apply, please submit a long abstract (between 500 and 700 words) via the dedicated form by December 27. Submissions are welcome from all technical, legal, economic, and sociological perspectives, as long as they are related to the theme of the workshop. The event will be an interactive half-day workshop, featuring in-depth discussions after each presentation.
Please note that the workshop will not involve the publication of scholarly articles for selected contributions, as it adopts a more practical, hands-on approach.

Submissions

Papers are to be submitted electronically through Google Form (https://forms.gle/vwQGqx1h5fZafLvW9).

  • Submissions must be in English, in pdf format.
  • Length: a submission must be of minimum 500 and maximum of 700 words. Unlimited pages are allowed for references.
  • Abstract information: the following information must appear in the first page of the submission:
    • The title of the paper,
    • A list of keywords

Presentation

Selected participants will be required to present the topic outlined in their long abstract, with each presentation lasting approx. 7-10 minutes, including slides and any supplementary material. For every accepted WORK at least one author is expected to register (as a regular registration) for the conference and present the work on-site in Bologna.

The "Cyber Threats' Evolution (ACTE)" workshop focuses on emerging cyber threats and innovative defense strategies, aligning with Italy's National Cybersecurity Strategy (2022-2026). It enables Ph.D. researchers in cybersecurity to present findings, collaborate, and share insights with experts, advancing solutions to anticipate and counter cyber threats.
